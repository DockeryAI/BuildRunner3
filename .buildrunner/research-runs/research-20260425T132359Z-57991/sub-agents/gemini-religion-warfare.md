## Hypotheses

### H1: The religious practices of henge builders were complex, involving astronomical alignments, ancestor veneration, and large-scale communal feasting
**Verdict:** CONFIRMED
**Surprise level:** Expected

### H2: Large-scale organized warfare in the Neolithic is likely overstated — evidence points more towards localized raiding
**Verdict:** PARTIAL
**Surprise level:** Somewhat Surprising — evidence reveals more organized violence than expected; massacre at Crickley Hill and construction of defensive enclosures suggest warfare was significant.

### H3: Bell Beaker population replacement was complex — not a simple swift military conquest
**Verdict:** CONFIRMED
**Surprise level:** Expected

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Henges Were Theaters for Ritual, Feasting, and Honoring the Dead
- **Detail:** Durrington Walls (2.8 km from Stonehenge) shows evidence of massive-scale feasting. Analysis of pig bones: slaughtered around 9 months of age, suggesting mid-winter festivals. Over 38,000 animal bone fragments found in a small excavated area. Isotopic analysis of cattle teeth shows animals came from as far away as northern England and Scotland. Prevailing theory: Durrington Walls = domain of the living (wooden structures); Stonehenge = domain of the dead/ancestors (permanent stone). People traveled long distances for these ritual events.
- **Source:** [UCL Feasting in Neolithic Britain](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/news/2007/jan/feasting-land-living-neolithic-britain); [Heritagedaily Durrington Walls](https://www.heritagedaily.com/2020/09/new-research-reveals-secrets-of-stonehenges-sister-monument/135248); [Cambridge Antiquity](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/antiquity/article/permanent-and-the-transitory-in-the-neolithic-landscapes-of-britain/68051878A3317765955E96614131583C)
- **Credibility:** [Peer-Reviewed]
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Finding 2: Astronomical Alignments Were a Core Component of Religion
- **Detail:** Major henge monuments show deliberate alignments with significant solar events, particularly solstices. Stonehenge main axis: aligned with midwinter sunset and midsummer sunrise. Woodhenge and Avebury also possess significant solar/lunar alignments. These suggest a cosmology deeply connected to seasonal cycles. Monuments may have served as calendars, ceremonial backdrops for seasonal rituals, or a means of demonstrating connection between earthly authority and celestial order. Precision indicates sophisticated astronomical observation over many generations.
- **Source:** [Cambridge News on Stonehenge-sun connection](https://www.cambridge.org/core/news/stonehenges-special-connection-to-the-sun-was-for-everyone-not-just-for-elites)
- **Credibility:** [Peer-Reviewed]
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Finding 3: Organized Violence and Massacres Were a Reality in the Neolithic
- **Detail:** Crickley Hill causewayed enclosure (Gloucestershire): major attack and massacre c. 3600-3500 BCE. Site defended by ditch and palisade which was breached. Over 400 flint arrowheads concentrated around entrances — a large-scale archery assault. Settlement was then systematically burned. Human remains in the ditch include two adult skulls with arrowhead injuries. "Sweet Track Man" (c. 3800 BCE Somerset): flint arrowhead embedded in spine, possible traumatic death from a club. Mounting evidence has shifted the scholarly consensus from a peaceful Neolithic toward a view of endemic violence and social hierarchy.
- **Source:** [Heritagedaily Crickley Hill](https://www.heritagedaily.com/2023/07/the-crickley-hill-massacre-a-neolithic-genocide/147987); [Heritagedaily Neolithic Violence](https://www.heritagedaily.com/2021/04/violence-in-the-neolithic/138865)
- **Credibility:** [Community]
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Finding 4: Bell Beaker Replacement — Genetic Evidence Clear, Mechanism Debated
- **Detail:** ~90% of Britain's gene pool replaced after 2450 BCE by Bell Beaker/Yamnaya steppe-derived peoples. The archaeological record does NOT show evidence of a single massive island-wide conflict. Debate: violent aggressive takeover (new weapon types, warrior ideology) vs. more complex model (demographic/technological advantage, gradual displacement with intermittent localized violence over a prolonged period). The lack of widespread "battlefield" archaeology lends credence to the complex model.
- **Source:** [NHM News](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/news/2018/february/large-scale-migration-into-britain-happened-after-stonehenge-was-built.html); [Olalde 2018 Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature25738)
- **Credibility:** [Official/Peer-Reviewed]
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## Metrics & Benchmarks
- Population replacement: ~90% gene pool replaced post-2450 BCE
- Crickley Hill arrowheads: >400 at site entrances
- Durrington Walls feasting: >38,000 animal bone fragments from small excavated area
- Pigs at Durrington Walls: slaughtered ~9 months old (mid-winter festival timing)
- Cattle isotope evidence: animals traveled from northern England and Scotland to Stonehenge environs
- Start of henge building: c. 3000 BCE
- Bell Beaker arrival: c. 2450 BCE

## Gotchas & Edge Cases
- "Henge" definition: specifically a circular/oval earthen enclosure with bank OUTSIDE the ditch. Stonehenge is technically not a true henge — it's a stone circle within such an enclosure. The bank-and-ditch configuration is clearly non-defensive and therefore ritualistic.
- Without written records, precise beliefs, myths, and social structures remain a matter of inference.
- Regional variation in monument styles, pottery (Grooved Ware vs Peterborough Ware), and burial practices is substantial — major sites in southern Britain are not representative of all of Neolithic Britain.

## Contradictions Found
- **Peaceful vs. violent Neolithic**: Now largely resolved toward endemic violence, but debate continues about nature and scale — raiding vs. organized warfare, ritual conflict vs. total war.
- **Nature of Bell Beaker "invasion"**: Violent aggressive takeover (new weapons, warrior ideology) vs. complex gradual replacement (demographic/technological advantage, prolonged process).
